I often get a lot of questions about curl's legal status and what requirements it defines. This is just a short summary:

    Curl and libcurl are true Open Source/Free Software and meet all definitions as such. It means that you are free to modify and redistribute all contents of the curl distributed archives.

    You may also freely use curl and libcurl in your commercial projects.

Curl and libcurl are licensed under a MIT/X derivate license or the Mozilla Public License, the MPL. Try the MPL license FAQ. More about the dual license in that separate section.

Curl and libcurl does not contain any GPL source. I don't agree with the "viral" aspects of GPL. Another reason it doesn't contain GPL source is that it would limit users of libcurl.

For many of the most common questions regarding curl and using curl with your [differently licensed] program, have a look at the license section of the FAQ.
